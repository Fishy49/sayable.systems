Put it on your device

Sayable runs right in the browser, but the best move is saving it to a home screen. After that it opens like any other app, full-screen, with no internet needed, and everything stays on your device.

  1. 1 🚀

    Open the app

    On the device you'll actually use (a tablet is perfect), open Sayable in the browser.

    Open the app

  2. 2 📲

    Save it to your home screen

    • iPhone & iPad: in Safari, tap the Share button, then Add to Home Screen.
    • Android: in Chrome, tap the ⋮ menu, then Add to Home screen (or Install app).
    • Computer: click the install icon at the right end of the address bar.
  3. 3 🎉

    That's it, it's yours

    From now on it opens from its own icon and works completely offline. Boards live on the device. No account to lose, nothing to renew.

Once saved, Sayable keeps working even without this website: the whole app lives on your device, not on a server. Set up boards in Edit mode, and use Settings → Export any time to keep a backup file of a profile.

Good questions

Frequently asked

What is AAC?

AAC stands for Augmentative and Alternative Communication: tools that help people who don't speak, or don't speak reliably, to communicate. A picture board that talks, like Sayable, is one common kind.

Is it really free? Where's the catch?

Really free. There's no account to create, nothing to buy, no ads, and no “upgrade” screen. Sayable is open-source software licensed under GPL-3.0, built to stay free.

Do I need internet?

Only for the first load. Save Sayable to your home screen and it runs completely offline afterward: the speech comes from your device, and your boards are stored on it. Perfect for cars, planes, and spotty Wi-Fi.

How do I put it on a tablet?

Open the app in the tablet's browser, then add it to the home screen. The setup steps walk through it for iPad, Android, and computers. It takes about a minute, and afterward Sayable opens from its own icon like any other app.

Which devices work?

Any device with a modern browser: iPad, Android tablet, Chromebook, phone, laptop. Tablets are the most popular because the tiles are big and tappable, but there's no special hardware to buy.

Can I use my own pictures?

Yes. Tiles can use emoji, searchable ARASAAC pictograms, or your own uploaded photos. Everything is stored right on the device so it keeps working offline.

Is my data private?

Completely. There's no server and no account, so boards never leave the device unless you choose to export a file. This website has no analytics or third-party trackers, either.

How do the voices work?

Sayable uses the voices already built into your device through the browser's Web Speech API. You can pick a voice and speaking speed per profile. Available voices vary by device and operating system.

Is this medical advice or a device recommendation?

No. Sayable is a free tool to make trying AAC easy. For choosing the right long-term system, please work with a speech-language pathologist. Sayable is a great, no-cost way to explore alongside them.

How can I help?

Use it, share it with a family or clinic who could use it, and, if you write code, the project is open on GitHub. Bug reports and ideas are welcome.
