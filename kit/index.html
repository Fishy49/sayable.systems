The Kiosk Kit

A dedicated talker,
from a tablet you can afford

Lock an inexpensive Samsung tablet down to just Sayable — no way out, no distractions, a voice your child picks. A QR code does the hard part. No computer, no accounts, about half an hour.

  • 🕐 ~30 minutes
  • 💶 Tablet + ~€9, one-time
  • 🖥️ No computer
  • 🔒 Really locked
This is the path we use and recommend — not the only one. It’s the simplest way we’ve found to lock a tablet with no computer. If you already use a device-management tool or another kiosk app, Sayable runs happily in those too. Pick whatever gets your kid talking.

Step 1

Pick a tablet

We test on Samsung’s Galaxy Tab line. Any of these work — choose by budget and how long you want updates.

Tablet Rough price Updates until
Galaxy Tab A11+ (11″)Lasts longest $220–250 new ~2032
Galaxy Tab A9+ (11″)Best value $90–150 refurb Oct 2027
Galaxy Tab A9 / A11 (8.7″) ~$100–140 2027 / ~2032

Skip the Tab A7 Lite (no more security updates). Wi-Fi-only models are fine. A rugged case with a strap is the best $20 you’ll spend here. You’ll also want your Wi-Fi password handy and a phone to show the QR code on.

Step 2

Start fresh

The setup only works on a factory-fresh tablet. Brand new in the box? Jump to step 3.

⚠️ Order matters here — the one place it really does. Remove the accounts before you reset. Otherwise Android’s anti-theft lock (Factory Reset Protection) can leave you stuck at a sign-in wall afterward.
  1. Settings → Accounts and backup → Manage accounts — remove the Google account.
  2. Remove the Samsung account too (it’ll ask for its password).
  3. Settings → General management → Reset → Factory data reset. Confirm, and let it restart to the “Welcome” screen.

Step 3

Here’s your QR code

One code for everyone — nothing personal in it. It tells the tablet what to install, to lock to Sayable, and which settings to use.

💡 Turn your screen brightness up before scanning. Reading this on the tablet you’re about to reset? Open this page on a phone instead.

Step 4

            
Scan, then follow along

            

              Five to ten minutes, start to finish. Here’s each thing you’ll see — including the couple of
              screens that look scarier than they are.
            

          


          

            
1 · Six taps on the Welcome screen

            

              Don’t start setup — just tap the same empty spot six times, about twice a
              second. A QR scanner opens.
            

            
          


          

            
2 · Scan the code & join Wi-Fi

            

              Point the camera at the QR code from step 3. The tablet connects to Wi-Fi and downloads the
              kiosk app — just say yes as it goes.
            

            

              👀 You’ll be asked to confirm the device is managed by “Fully Kiosk Browser”,
              not private. That’s expected — it’s exactly what makes the lock real. Tap to continue.
            

          


          

            
3 · A “Provisioning Code” box appears → tap Skip

            

              This is asking for a paid cloud account we don’t use. Just tap Skip — our
              settings come from the QR code, not from there.
            

            
          


          

            
4 · Grant a few permissions

            

              The app asks for permissions one screen at a time. This part is a little clunky, so here’s the
              rhythm:
            

            
    
              
  1. Tap Get Permissions (or Allow).
    2. 
              
  2. Flip the toggle on, or tap Allow.
    3. 
              
  3. 
                Tap the small ◀ back arrow in the bottom corner to return — and repeat
                until you land back on the app’s screen.
              
    4. 
            

            

              💡 The back arrow is the trick. Each Allow drops you on a system page; the ◀ arrow brings you
              back for the next one. A handful of rounds and you’re done.
            

          


          

            
5 · It lands on Sayable, locked 🎉

            

              The tablet restarts straight into Sayable — no home button, no way out, no Google sign-in and
              no account created. You may see a small watermark from the kiosk app; step 5 clears it.
            

            
          

        

      


      
      

        

          

            
Step 5

            
Make it yours

            

              Three quick things, all on the tablet. To reach settings anytime:
              swipe in from the left edge, then type the PIN — it starts as 1234.
            

          


          

            
Change the PIN first

            

              Everyone reading this knows “1234.” Swipe from the left → 1234 → Settings → Kiosk
              Mode → Kiosk Exit PIN → pick your own.
            

          

          

            
Buy the license (~€9, once)

            

              Clears the watermark. Swipe from the left → PIN → Get a PLUS License in the
              menu → pay. Lifetime, no account, and it moves to a new tablet if you ever replace this one.
            

          

          

            
Pick their voice, add their words

            

              In Sayable’s caregiver settings, try the voices together — a dozen-plus English, hundreds more.
              Finding “their” voice is the whole point. Then build the words that matter in your child’s world.
            

          

        

      


      
      

        

          

            
Everyday

            
Living with it

          

          

            

              It turns on into Sayable and stays there. Speaking works with no internet once it’s loaded —
              just keep it charged. To restart or power off, swipe from the left, enter your
              PIN, and use the tablet’s normal controls — that’s the grown-up door.
            

            

              Every few months: exit with your PIN and check Settings → Software update
              (Samsung sends these with no account). Sayable itself updates on its own — a little “reload”
              note shows up when it does.
            

          

        

      


      
      

        

          

            
Help

            
If something’s off

          


          

            

              “Can’t set up device” during setup
              

                Usually shaky Wi-Fi mid-download. Let the tablet reset itself and start again from the six
                taps — nothing’s broken, and this step is always safe to repeat.
              

            

            

              It asks for a Google account you removed
              

                That’s the anti-theft lock — the account wasn’t removed before the reset. Sign in with that
                Google account once to clear it, then redo step 2 in order: remove accounts first, then reset.
              

            

            

              The six taps don’t open a scanner
              

                Be on the very first screen (language pick) and tap the same spot six times, about twice a
                second. Very old tablets need Wi-Fi first — connect, then try again.
              

            

            

              Setup finished but it’s not locked to Sayable
              

                Something hiccupped mid-setup. The clean fix is to run the whole thing again: factory reset
                and re-scan the QR code. It only takes a few minutes, and your license stays attached to the
                tablet.
              

            

            

              I forgot the exit PIN
              

                It’s not recoverable — that’s the point of it. Factory reset and run the kit again (no
                accounts to remove this time; the setup never made any). Your license re-attaches, and if you
                exported a backup in Sayable’s settings, your boards come right back.
              

            

            

              Voices are missing or sound robotic
              

                Exit with your PIN → Settings → General management → Text-to-speech → Google
                → install the voice data, then restart. No account needed.
