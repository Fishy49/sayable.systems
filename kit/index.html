The Kiosk Kit

Turn an affordable tablet into a
dedicated talker

A dedicated communication device — one that only runs Sayable, can’t be escaped by curious fingers, and speaks in a voice your child chooses — normally costs $500 to $6,000. This free guide builds one from an off-the-shelf Samsung tablet. No computer. No accounts. No technical skills. Our QR code does the hard part.

  • 🕐 About 30 minutes
  • 💶 Tablet + ~€9 software, one-time
  • 🖥️ No computer needed
  • 🔒 Real device-level lockdown
1 · Get a tablet 2 · Reset it safely 3 · The QR code 4 · Scan & wait 5 · Make it yours

Step 1

Get a tablet

The kit currently supports Samsung’s Galaxy Tab line (that’s what we test on). Any of these works — pick by budget and how long you want security updates.

Tablet Rough price Updates until Notes
Galaxy Tab A11+ (11″)Longevity pick $220–250 new ~2032 Samsung promises 7 years of security updates. Best if this device should last a childhood.
Galaxy Tab A9+ (11″)Budget pick $90–150 refurbished, ~$200 new Oct 2027 Great value; the tablet this kit was developed on. Honest caveat: updates end in 2027.
Galaxy Tab A9 / A11 (8.7″) ~$100–140 2027 / ~2032 Smaller and lighter — nice for small hands, fewer words per screen.

Skip the Galaxy Tab A7 Lite — it no longer receives security updates. Wherever you buy, Wi-Fi-only models are fine. A rugged kid-proof case with a handle or strap is the best $20 you’ll spend on this project.

You’ll also need

  • Your home Wi-Fi name and password.
  • A phone or computer to show this page’s QR code on (the tablet scans it from that screen).
  • About €9 (~$10) later for the kiosk software’s one-time license — paid on the tablet itself.

Step 2

Reset the tablet safely

The setup magic only works on a tablet that’s factory-fresh. Brand-new in the box? Skip straight to step 3.

⚠️ Do this in order — it matters. Android has an anti-theft feature (Factory Reset Protection) that can lock a tablet after a reset if accounts are still on it. Removing the accounts first keeps it disarmed. This is the only step in the whole guide where order matters this much.

On the tablet you’re reusing

  1. Open Settings → Accounts and backup → Manage accounts and remove the Google account (if any).
  2. Remove the Samsung account too (it asks for its password). If Find My Mobile was on, this switches it off.
  3. Now reset: Settings → General management → Reset → Factory data reset, and confirm. The tablet restarts to a “Welcome” screen.

Step 3

Pull up the QR code

One code, same for everyone — there’s nothing personal inside it. It tells the tablet: install the kiosk app, lock to Sayable, apply our tested settings. You’ll pick your Wi-Fi on the tablet during setup, just like any new device.

💡 Turn this screen’s brightness up when scanning. If you’re reading this on the tablet you’re about to reset — open this page on a phone instead.

Step 4

            
Six taps, one scan, done

            
Here’s exactly what you’ll see. The whole thing takes 5–10 minutes.

          


          

            
1Wake the fresh tablet to its “Welcome” screen

            
The first screen usually asks for your language. Don’t tap “Start” or pick anything.

          

          

            
2Tap the same empty spot 6 times, quickly

            

              Tap-tap-tap-tap-tap-tap on the welcome screen. A camera/QR scanner opens — this is Android’s
              built-in setup-by-QR feature.
            

          

          

            
3Scan the QR code from step 3

            

              Point the tablet’s camera at the code on your phone or computer screen. Along the way the
              tablet asks you to join your Wi-Fi — connect just like on any new device.
            

          

          

            
4Let it work

            
You’ll see progress screens like:

            
“Getting ready for work setup…” · “Setting up your device…”

            

              Don’t worry about the office-speak — Android uses its business machinery for this. The tablet
              downloads the kiosk app (Fully Kiosk Browser), makes it the device’s owner, applies our
              settings, and restarts into Sayable, locked and ready. No Google sign-in ever
              appears, and no account is created.
            

          

        

      


      
      

        

          

            
Step 5

            
Make it yours

            

              Three finishing touches, all on the tablet. To reach the kiosk’s settings at any time:
              swipe in from the left edge, then enter the PIN — 1234 to start.
            

          


          

            
1Change the exit PIN (please!)

            

              Every kit tablet starts with PIN 1234, and everyone who reads this page knows
              it. Swipe in from the left edge → enter 1234 → Settings → Kiosk Mode → Kiosk Exit
              PIN → set your own. Do this first.
            

          

          

            
2Buy the kiosk license (~€9, one-time)

            

              The kiosk software runs free with a watermark. To remove it: swipe in from the left edge →
              PIN → Get a PLUS License in the menu → pay. It’s a lifetime license for this
              tablet, transferable if the tablet is ever replaced, and needs no account.
            

          

          

            
3Pick their voice, build their words

            

              In Sayable, open the caregiver settings and try the voices — there are a dozen-plus English
              ones (and 200 in other languages). Finding “their” voice is the whole point; take your time
              together. Then add the words and pictures that matter in your child’s world.
            

          

        

      


      
      

        

          

            
Ongoing

            
Living with it

            
Almost nothing. That’s the point of a dedicated device.

          


          

            
Every day

            

              It turns on into Sayable. It stays in Sayable. Speaking works without internet once it has
              loaded. Charge it like anything else.
            

          

          

            
A few times a year

            
    
              
  • 
                Android updates: exit the kiosk (swipe from left + PIN) →
                Settings app → Software update. Samsung delivers these without any account.
              
    • 
              
  • 
                Sayable updates: arrive automatically from the web — a small “reload”
                banner appears in the app when one is ready.

  •

Help

            
If something goes sideways

          


          

            

              The tablet says “Can’t set up device” during setup
              

                Android’s way of saying the download or install hiccupped (often shaky Wi-Fi). The tablet
                will offer to reset — let it, and run the setup again from the 6 taps. Nothing is damaged;
                this step is always safely repeatable.
              

            

            

              After a reset it demands “the Google account previously synced on this device”
              

                That’s Factory Reset Protection — the account wasn’t removed before resetting. Sign in with
                that Google account once to unlock, then follow step 2 in the right order: remove accounts
                first, then reset.
              

            

            

              The 6-tap trick doesn’t open a scanner
              

                Make sure you’re on the very first setup screen (language selection) and tap 6 times in the
                same blank spot, about two taps per second. On very old Android versions the tablet needs
                internet first — connect to Wi-Fi in the wizard, then try again.
              

            

            

              Setup finished but the tablet isn’t locked to Sayable
              

                The settings download likely failed mid-setup. Open the Fully Kiosk app → menu → Settings →
                Other Settings → Import Settings from URL and enter
                https://sayable.systems/kit/fully-settings.json — then restart the tablet.
              

            

            

              I forgot my exit PIN
              

                The kiosk PIN isn’t recoverable from the lock screen — that’s its job. If it’s truly lost,
                factory reset (step 2 has no accounts to remove this time — the kit setup never creates any)
                and run the kit again; your license re-attaches to the same tablet, and Sayable boards can be
                restored from a backup file if you exported one in caregiver settings.
              

            

            

              The voices are missing or it speaks with a robot voice
              

                The tablet’s Google speech engine may need voice data. Exit the kiosk (swipe from left +
                PIN) → Settings app → General management → Text-to-speech → Google engine → install voice
                data, then reboot. No account is needed for voice downloads.
